How to Spot Makeup & Cosmetics Scams in 2026

By scam.makeup | February 21, 2026 | 10 min read

Counterfeit cosmetics generate over $5.4 billion in illegal revenue annually. Fake makeup products contain dangerous chemicals including lead, mercury, arsenic, and bacteria that cause serious health problems.

Top Makeup Scams in 2026

  • Counterfeit luxury brands — fake MAC, NARS, Charlotte Tilbury on TikTok Shop and Amazon
  • Influencer pay-to-promote — undisclosed paid promotions for unsafe products
  • AI deepfake tutorials — fake celebrity makeup tutorials promoting scam products
  • Subscription box fraud — copycat services sending expired or counterfeit samples
  • Fake "organic" certifications — products claiming certifications they don't have

Red Flags

  • Price 50%+ below retail — almost certainly counterfeit
  • No batch code or lot number on packaging
  • Spelling errors on labels or packaging
  • Unusual smell, texture, or color compared to authentic product
  • Shipped from overseas in non-branded packaging
